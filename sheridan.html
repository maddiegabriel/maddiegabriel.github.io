About Sheridan



Sheridan College is one of the leading post-secondary institutions in Canada. Over 25000 students choose to study at Sheridan each year! This Canadian college was founded in Brampton in 1967 and is famous for its arts and technology programs such as animation and illustration.

Sheridan has three campuses across Ontario - in Brampton, Mississauga and Oakville. I worked from the Trafalgar campus in Oakville for my co-op term. My office - known as the Tutoring Centre - is located in the Library Learning Commons. The Tutoring Centre is an incredible free resource for Sheridan students. It is home to tutors who are there to support students as they study various subjects, from Math to English.

I chose to work at Sheridan because I was interested in trying my hand at teaching and wanted to see how I would enjoy it!

Semester Goals

Tech Literacy

My goal was to learn more about the tech I work with and become an expert in the voice area. I worked hard at all my dev tasks, got over the steep learning curve and expanded my knowledge. I wasn't shy to ask for help from more senior devs when I got stuck. I went from knowing virtually nothing about voice technology to writing functional code for Sun Life and can confidently build my own apps.

Creativity

I hoped to exercise my creativity by working on some Innovation projects at Sun Life. Luckily my dev role allowed me the freedom to explore and prototype new ideas. I managed my time to fit this work into my schedule between contributing to delivery-stream dev work. I worked on 6 different prototypes over my work term, at all stages from brainstorming to designing a prototype to pitching!

Communication

A goal was to become more comfortable communicating my ideas to an audience. Even as a developer, I got lots of practice through volunteering to do product demos. I learned to present our apps/prototypes to technical and non-technical stakeholders. I also worked on being more vocal in meetings and explaining my ideas clearly. I also got lots of French practice as the only French-speaking team member!

Teamwork

As part of the Wildcards dev team, I hoped to improve my teamwork skills and transfer my agile team experience to future jobs. I observed team practices to see how to best contribute as a new member. I went to agile training and participated fully in all team ceremonies. I got along super well with everyone and was always open to collaborate on design/dev work. We worked together smoothly + had a lot of fun!


Conclusion

After completing this co-op work term, I can say without a doubt that the co-op program is extremely valuable - especially for B.Comp students. My co-op allowed me to take the foundational knowledge I learn in school and apply it to a real-world job with clients and deadlines. It's a nice confidence boost to affirm that I can succeed at a job in my field, which motivates me to continue growing. My experience at Sun Life exceeded my expectations; I had no idea that working at an insurance company would be so cool and fun! Returning to school this semester, I have gained valuable knowledge and perspective on how I wish to focus the rest of my degree!
