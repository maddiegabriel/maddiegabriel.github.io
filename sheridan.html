About Sheridan



Sheridan College is one of the leading post-secondary institutions in Canada. Over 25 000 students choose to study at Sheridan each year! This Canadian college was founded in Brampton in 1967 and is famous for its arts and technology programs such as animation and illustration.

Sheridan has three campuses across Ontario - in Brampton, Mississauga and Oakville. I worked from the Trafalgar campus in Oakville for my co-op term. My office - known as the Tutoring Centre - is located in the Library Learning Commons. The Tutoring Centre is an incredible free resource for Sheridan students. It is home to student tutors (like me) whose job is to support students as they study various subjects, from Math to English to Programming.

I chose to work at Sheridan because I was interested in trying my hand at a teaching-related co-op and wanted to see how I would enjoy it!

My Role

Java Learning Assistant


Overview
My main responsibility during this work term was to work one-on-one with my students daily and assist them in their learning. This includeds explaining concepts, helping with assignments/debugging, and going beyond the course content. Altough my title is a Java Learning Assistant, I helped my students with many other programming courses. I was also responsible for running three 'Java Jam' tutorials every week. Lastly, I completed many side-projects to help improve the Tutoring Centre's services.

Main Tasks
1) Meet with students in the Tutoring Centre for Appointments and Drop-In sessions

  • Explain course concepts to students using different teaching strategies
  • Help students understand and break down their programming assignments
  • Aid students in debugging their code (Java, C, PHP, Javascript, HTML/CSS, SQL, etc.)
  • Show students how to use different technologies (IDE's, command line, git, debugger, etc.)
  • Give students helpful advice on how to succeed in their courses/exams
  • Communicate with the professors of my different course sections
  • Keep up with students' course material so I can assist them better
  • Provide personalized practice/homework material for students
  • Manage my time effectively so my appointments don't go overtime and the centre runs smoothly
  • Record notes for each meeting and add this data to Microsoft SharePoint database
2) Run three weekly 'Java Jam' tutorials for the 'Object Oriented Programming 2' course
  • Attend three hour-long tutorials per week, with about 20 students each
  • Prepare and present engaging powerpoints based on the current course content
  • Review students' quizzes and provide explanations/re-grades as necessary
  • Answer any questions from students about the course content or assignments
  • Work with individual students who require extra help
3) Side projects to improve the Sheridan Tutoring Centre
  • Used Excel & FireApps to pull student data from SharePoint to create a weekly stats report
  • Sent the weekly report to management and answer any questions
  • Enhanced the Tutoring Centre's SharePoint data collection system (new features)
  • Created & distributed marketing materials (posters, flyers etc.) for the centre
  • Did class visits to get to know students face-to-face and encourage them to attend Java Jams
  • Ran a Java Exam Cram review session in Week 6, before the students' midterm
  • Created a Java Fundamentals Package based on students' lecture notes and textbook
  • Created and presented a Java Review Session to catch up after reading week

Semester Goals

Teaching Skills

I chose Sheridan to challenge myself with a new type of co-op and improve my teaching skills. My main goal was to assess/improve my ability to convey information to novice programmers. I tried to be self-aware when assisting students, recognizing the effectiveness of my own teaching strategies. I paid close attention to students’ responses to see if they were understanding. I prepared for my appointments, allowing me to provide a better tutoring experience. I think I was an effective teacher as my students frequently returned for more appointments; my overall return rate was 75%. While I improved at teaching, I also saw my students become better programmers over the semester!

Tech Literacy

My goal was to expand my technical skills by learning more about the technology my students are working with. Explaining programming concepts requires an in-depth understanding of the content. I feel I mastered the Advanced OOP fundamentals and gained a deeper understanding of Java details by reading the prof-recommended Head First Java book. By the end of my work-term, I could answer/work through all of my students' questions about their course content. I also learned to build custom statistics software using SharePoint/FireApps. I built features to help ease the Tutoring Centre's data collection process and connect it to Excel reports for management.

Communication

In my role as a Java LA, I spent most of my time working one-on-one with students or running Java Jams for larger groups of up to 20 students. Through these activities, I achieved my goal of improving my oral communication skills. I paid attention to how I communicated with students, including my tone and body language, and adjusted these behaviours according to their response and specific needs. By the end of my work-term, I felt completely comfortable teaching my students both individually and in groups. I made connections with many individual students who returned to the centre frequently for help, which proves that I was successful in communicating concepts to them.

Leadership

I hoped to improve my leadership skills this work-term by leading three weekly Java Jam tutorials and by taking initiative to improve the Tutoring Centre services. I think I became an approachable leader figure to my Java Jam students, who often came to me for valuable and knowledgeable guidance/assistance. I feel my students showed me respect and were confident in the assistance and advice I provided them. I also brainstormed & implemented many new ideas to better help students in the Tutoring Centre, which I listed above in the 'My Role' section of this report. I also took initiative to market our services across campus using posters and class visits!


Conclusion

My work term in the Tutoring Centre at Sheridan showed me once again how valuable the co-op program at Guelph is. Through my teaching role, I was able to practice valuable soft skills while also deepening my knowledge of many computer science concepts by explaining them to others. I had a great time trying my hand at teaching; I felt like I helped my students every day, and went home feeling content! I made valuable connections with my co-workers, professors and my students, who were from all parts of the world and in all different stages of life. As I return to Guelph this Fall semester, I feel I have gained valuable knowledge, skills and experience which I will apply to my future learning!
